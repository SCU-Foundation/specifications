# Code of Conduct — Lattice Identity Token Specification (LIT‑Spec)

**Specification:** Lattice Identity Token Specification (LIT‑Spec)  
**Version:** v1.0  
**Steward:** SCU Foundation  
**Research Body:** Lattice Research Institute (LRI)  
**Repository:** REPO: Specifications — /specifications/lit/

---

## Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the behavioural expectations for all contributors participating in the stewardship, maintenance, or discussion of this specification.  
It ensures that all work remains neutral, safe, professional, and aligned with the constitutional identity, provenance, safety, and attestation principles defined by the SCU Foundation.

---

## Guiding Principles

Contributors MUST:

- act with professionalism, respect, and integrity  
- maintain neutrality and avoid conflicts of interest  
- uphold identity, provenance, attestation, safety, and boundary principles  
- avoid introducing proprietary, product‑specific, or commercial bias  
- respect the governance authority of the SCU Foundation  
- respect the research independence of the Lattice Research Institute (LRI)  
- ensure all contributions align with canonical repository structure and governed update sequences  
- follow the SCU Foundation’s interoperability, safety, and identity‑boundary rules  

---

## Expected Behaviour

Participants MUST:

- engage constructively and respectfully  
- provide clear, evidence‑based reasoning  
- follow the SCU Foundation’s change‑management and review processes  
- maintain confidentiality for non‑public governance deliberations  
- avoid personal attacks, harassment, or discriminatory behaviour  
- avoid misrepresentation of the specification or governance model  
- collaborate in good faith and avoid obstructive behaviour  

---

## Unacceptable Behaviour

Prohibited behaviours include:

- harassment, discrimination, or abusive conduct  
- introducing proprietary or commercial influence into normative content  
- bypassing governance or change‑management processes  
- misusing the specification for endorsement or promotional purposes  
- submitting misleading, harmful, or ambiguous proposals  
- violating licensing terms or misrepresenting attribution  
- disregarding canonical update sequences or governance decisions

## Enforcement

The SCU Foundation is responsible for:

- interpreting this Code of Conduct  
- reviewing alleged violations  
- determining actions or sanctions  
- maintaining neutrality and ecosystem integrity  

Possible actions include warnings, revision requests, participation restrictions, or removal from governance processes.
